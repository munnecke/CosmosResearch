The Hubble tension — a persistent discrepancy between how fast the universe appears to be expanding depending on whether you measure from the early or late cosmos — is one of the most interesting open problems in cosmology.

Our working hypothesis is bold: the tension isn't a measurement error. It's a **real physical signal** of a cosmological phase transition.

## The approach

Rather than fitting a single parametric model, we're using AI-driven symbolic regression to explore a broad space of possible dark energy evolution models, *w(z)*. The idea is to let the math discover functional forms we might not think to try.

We test across three independent probes:

- **Pantheon+ SN Ia** — type Ia supernovae as standardizable candles
- **DESI BAO** — baryon acoustic oscillation measurements
- **Planck CMB** — cosmic microwave background constraints

Multi-probe consistency is key. A transition model that only fits one dataset isn't very convincing.

## Early results

The experiments are still running, but the framework is producing interesting candidates for transition redshifts *z\** that merit closer examination. More to come as we refine the search and run ablation studies.

## What's next

We're currently running ablation experiments to understand which components of the model are doing the heavy lifting. Stay tuned for a deeper writeup once the dust settles.
