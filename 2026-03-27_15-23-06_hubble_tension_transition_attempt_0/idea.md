## Name

hubble_tension_transition

## Title

Agentic Exploration of Cosmological Phase Transitions in the Hubble Tension

## Short Hypothesis

The Hubble tension indicates a real physical signal of a cosmological phase transition, which can be detected through AI-driven exploration of transition redshifts using diverse astrophysical datasets.

## Related Work

Previous studies have proposed various dark energy models to explain the Hubble tension, often constrained to specific parametrizations like CPL. This work diverges by employing symbolic regression to uncover novel functional forms of dark energy evolution, and tests consistency across multiple probes, a method not extensively explored in prior research.

## Abstract

This research investigates whether the Hubble tension—a significant discrepancy between early and late universe measurements—signals a cosmological phase transition. We hypothesize that the universe undergoes a smooth transition, affecting dark energy's equation of state. Utilizing AI-driven methods, we explore a broad hypothesis space for transition models in w(z) and H₀(z). The proposal leverages Pantheon+ SN Ia, DESI BAO, and Planck CMB datasets, with Bayesian evidence and multi-probe consistency as key evaluation metrics. This approach aims to identify a transition redshift z* and provide evidence for new cosmological physics beyond the ΛCDM model.

## Experiments

- Baseline fitting using ΛCDM and CPL models to establish reference metrics.
- Transition model search through grid and symbolic regression to identify best-fit w(z) forms.
- Multi-probe consistency tests across SN Ia, BAO, and CMB datasets.
- Symbolic regression to discover novel dark energy evolution models.
- Exploration of Hubble dipole anisotropy in supernovae data.

## Risk Factors And Limitations

- Potential overfitting in AI-driven models.
- Computational intensity of large-scale Bayesian model comparisons.
- Interpretability challenges in symbolic regression outcomes.
- Data limitations or biases affecting transition model detection.
